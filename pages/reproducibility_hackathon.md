The Chameleon team invites students to participate in a **Reproducibility Hackathon** at the [13th Greater Chicago Area Systems Research (GCASR) 2026 conference](https://gcasr.org/2026/). Participants will package experiments from their published research for reproducibility on Chameleon using [Trovi](http://trovi.chameleoncloud.org), review the artifacts of other participants, and the top contributors will present their work in a dedicated session at the workshop. 

> *Note: This event will take place concurrently with the poster session. If you are planning to participate in the **poster session**, please do **not** apply for this session, and vice versa.*

## What's Involved

Selected participants (“reproducibility ambassadors”) will do the following.

#### Before the conference

1. **Package their research:** Take your experiment from a recently published CS paper (last 3 years) and package it for reproducibility on Chameleon using Trovi ([trovi.chameleoncloud.org](trovi.chameleoncloud.org)). Record a video presenting your packaged experiment and reproducibility findings. See examples [here](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLF3MJ-XNj1_8i6lyDlboJ7qyEhaPkf8O2).
2. **Package a second paper:** Package an additional published paper (from a top conference in the last 3 years) that is not currently in the Trovi repository.
3. **Review others’ artifacts:** Reproduce and review 2 packaged experiments from other participants, documenting findings and any challenges encountered.

#### At the conference

The **top 6** packages and presentations will be selected to present during a dedicated 2-hour session at GCASR (15 minutes each: 10-minute presentation + 5-minute Q&A). Note: This session will take place at the same time as the poster session.
