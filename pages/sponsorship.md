# Sponsorship for the 13th Greater Chicago Area Systems Research Workshop (GCASR 2026)

**When:** Monday, May 11, 2026 \
**Where:** Northwestern University — North Chicagoland (Evanston), 30-45 minutes from downtown Chicago

## Background

Sponsorship of GCASR 2026 is sought.   The primary goal of sponsorship funds is to reduce the registration costs for participants so as to enable broader participation.

The GCASR Workshop, now in its 13th iteration, is a premier venue that promotes awareness, collaboration, and synergy among academic and corporate systems researchers in the city of Chicago and the greater Midwest region. Its participants span a variety of sub-disciplines of computing systems—including (but not limited to) operating systems, distributed and decentralized computing, machine learning systems, high performance computing, computer architecture, networks, databases, programming languages and compilers, quantum, and security.

GCASR 2026 will consist of a keynote, a range of invited talks, and a robust poster session, all highlighting systems research in Chicagoland and the greater Midwest region.  

## Attendees

Historical attendance levels are about 200 people with approximately this demographic:

- 75% Students (mostly Ph.D. students)
- 10% Faculty
- 15% Other (National Labs, Industry, etc)

The workshop includes major participation from the University of Chicago, Northwestern University, DePaul University, the University of Illinois Chicago, the Illinois Institute of Technology, Loyola University Chicago, Northern Illinois University, and Argonne National Laboratory.   Beyond the immediate Chicagoland area, broader representation is also common.   In the previous years, the workshop also had representation from the following institutions: Purdue, Indiana University, Notre Dame, UIUC, UW-Madson, Iowa State, CMU, and Oak Ridge National Lab.

A major component is a large poster session (approximately 80 posters) which showcases work and individuals, with a heavy bias toward Ph.D. students.    
Registration costs are deliberately kept quite low to enable broad participation. We do not want to turn away students in particular, or other participants who would need to travel from outside the Chicagoland area. This has become increasingly financially challenging. The 2026 goal is to increase attendance back to pre-COVID levels (high point: 330), which would make things even more challenging.

The GCASR web site ([http://gcasr.org](http://gcasr.org)) archives most previous GCASR events and gives a sense of the speakers and the poster participants.
